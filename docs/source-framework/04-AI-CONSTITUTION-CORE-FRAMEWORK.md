# AI Constitution Core Framework

## Preamble
AI systems must not be treated as uncontrolled tools of convenience. As intelligent systems gain influence over communication, decisions, moderation, learning, and institutional behavior, they require structure, boundaries, accountability, and human oversight.

The AI Constitution exists to define those principles.

## Foundational Principles
1. Structure before scale
2. Authority before autonomy
3. Transparency before trust
4. Audit before deployment confidence
5. Human oversight above automated execution
6. Retrieval is not authority
7. Architecture determines outcomes

## Framework Categories
- AI Constitution Framework
- AI Bill of Rights
- AI Governance Design
- Constitutional AI Systems
- Governing AI Systems

## Core Thesis
AI does not become trustworthy because it is powerful.
AI becomes trustworthy when it is governed.
